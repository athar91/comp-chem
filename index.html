Research Lines

🦠
Biophysics of Antimicrobial Resistance & RND Transporters
Molecular characterization of drug expulsion mechanisms in RND-superfamily efflux pumps (AcrB, MdtF, MexB/D/F/Y). Microsecond MD simulations revealed how single-residue mutations reshape the distal binding pocket and alter antibiotic specificity. Published in Nature Communications (2025 ×2) and npj Antimicrobials and Resistance (2026).
A01 · A03 · A04 · A12 · A16
⚙️
Computational Protocols & Methodology (gEDES)
Co-development and generalization of the EDES/gEDES protocol — metadynamics-based ensemble generation from apo structures for highly flexible and allosteric proteins. Validated on DUDe-38 dataset. Currently developing coarse-grained force fields and pocket-volume applications.
A05 · gEDES pipeline · Methods in Enzymology 2025
🔬
Protein Dynamics & Allosteric Regulation
"Rock and Lock" allosteric mechanism in S. pneumoniae Pyruvate Kinase (SpPYK) — effector binding locks the interface and propagates concerted motions 40Å to the catalytic center. Collaboration with Osaka University (Nishino/Taguchi lab). Manuscript communicated to J. Biol. Chemistry 2026.
U2 · In collaboration with Osaka University
💊
Medicinal Chemistry & Targeted Drug Discovery
Structure-based drug design spanning BRCT domain PPI interfaces, M. tuberculosis proteome, sirtuin inhibitors for triple-negative breast cancer, and viral proteases (SARS-CoV-2). SAR models, 3D-QSAR, pharmacophore modeling, and molecular docking pipelines.
A02 · A10 · A20 · A26 · A35 · A41
🔷
Supramolecular Chemistry & Host–Guest Systems
DFT and MD investigations of calixarenes, oxacalixarenes, and p-sulfocalixarenes as drug-delivery vehicles. Demonstrated enhanced solubility and anticancer activity of Genistein and Morin hydrate via host–guest inclusion complexation. Conformational analysis of complex macrocyclic architectures.
A07 · A13 · A24 · A31 · A32 · A45 · A50
⚛️
Electronic Structure & Nanomaterials
Electronic structure modeling of fullerene C60-based non-covalent dyads (IIT Madras, 2020–21). Applied DFT, EDA, QTAIM, and NBO analyses to explain through-bond-driven through-space interactions leading to dyadic fractal formation.
A17 · J. Phys. Chem. A 2022
